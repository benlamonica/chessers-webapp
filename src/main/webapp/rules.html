Moving
  • On the first turn, you may only move a maximum of 2 spaces
  • Squares can move 1-4 vertical spaces or 1-4 horizontal spaces
  • Triangles can move 1-3 diagonal spaces
  • Circles can move 1-2 spaces in any direction
  • Kings can move 1 space in any direction except when in check.
  • Kings may not move into check unless moving into the space will end the game.
  • When a king is in check:
    • A king may jump over any group of pieces to get out of check.
    • No other pieces can be moved unless they remove the king from check.
Capturing Pieces
  • Squares can only capture an opposing piece that is exactly 4 spaces away in a horizontal or vertical direction.
  • Triangles can only capture an opposing piece that is exactly 3 spaces away in a diagonal direction.
  • Circles can only capture an opposing piece that is exactly 2 spaces away.
  • Kings can only capture an opposing piece that is exactly directly adjacent.
Winning
  • Get your king from your corner to your opponent's corner
  • Checkmate your opponent's king
    • The opposing player will be unable to move, and must skip a turn.
    • When it is your turn again, you may capture the opponents king and end the game.
